Editorial teams don't think in database fields — they think in drafts, edits, and deadlines. Firelight started as a weekend prototype for a newsroom friend who was fighting her CMS more than her copy.

> The best CMS is the one the writer forgets they're using.

That line became the spec.

## Writing first, structure underneath

The editor reads like a manuscript, not a form. Versioning works like a track-changes conversation instead of a commit log. And the structured content — the fields, the schemas, the API — lives underneath, invisible until a developer needs it.

## My part

I designed the whole surface and half the API, working from the founding-designer seat: every screen, and enough of the data model to make sure the screens weren't lying about what the system could do.
