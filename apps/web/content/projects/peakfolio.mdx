Peakfolio started with a complaint I kept hearing from developers: years of work, and nowhere to put it that didn't feel like a template. Every portfolio builder on the market optimized for launch speed — and every result looked identical.

## The problem

Launch speed is the wrong thing to optimize when the whole point of a portfolio is to look like *you*. The builders all shipped the same three layouts with a color picker bolted on, so the sites they produced were interchangeable. That was the gap.

## What I built

A platform where the theming engine is the product. Design tokens run all the way down the stack, MDX essays are first-class citizens rather than an afterthought, and a procedural cover system means no two sites ship looking the same.

I led design and built the front end over eight months.

## Where it landed

Peakfolio reached public beta in spring 2025, with 4,000 sites live by the end of the year. The constraint-based layout engine at its core was later extracted into [Gridly](/projects/gridly/), which took on a life of its own.
